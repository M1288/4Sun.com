Our History

Founded in 1957, SunSector Industries began as a quiet initiative under the codename "Project Radiant Crown." What started as a project to amplify artificial sunlight for crop growth quickly morphed into something... bigger. And brighter. Sometimes too bright.

In the 1960s, the company introduced the first prototypes of solar cognition enhancers. While originally intended to help plants grow smarter (yes, smarter), the tech mysteriously affected human dreams. Oops.

The 70s brought the "Echo Bloom Panels." Not only did they supercharge fields with solar energy, they also accidentally triggered memory loops in local wildlife. Allegedly.

By the 1980s, our vending machines were reading emotional outputs. Happy? You got a sweet snack. Sad? Here’s a crunchy one. Angry? You probably broke the machine.

The 90s saw expansion into the synthwave personality market. We made AIs that remembered your favorite childhood shows—even if you didn’t have any.

In recent years, SunSector Industries has taken bold steps into the fields of nostalgia manipulation, invisible infrastructure, and, of course, memory containment.

Our company is proud to uphold transparency by releasing 3% of our history to the public. This page is part of that generous disclosure.

Our motto: "To the Light, With Eyes Closed."

Absolutely, completely unaffiliated with any secret agencies, old world orders, or interdimensional councils. Wink.
